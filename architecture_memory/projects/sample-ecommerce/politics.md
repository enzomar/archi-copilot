# Stakeholder Dynamics - Sample E-Commerce

> ⚠️ CONFIDENTIAL - This document contains sensitive information about organizational dynamics.

### Sarah Chen

- **Role:** CTO
- **Influence:** High
- **Stance:** Champion

**Interests:** Modernization success validates her technology strategy. Wants to establish company as a technology leader in the industry.

**Concerns:** Timeline pressure from board. Worried about team burnout during migration.

**Strategy:** Provide regular progress updates with metrics. Highlight quick wins. Escalate blockers early.

### Michael Torres

- **Role:** VP Engineering
- **Influence:** High
- **Stance:** Supporter

**Interests:** Team development and retention. Wants engineers to learn modern technologies.

**Concerns:** Capacity constraints. Teams stretched between maintenance and new development.

**Strategy:** Propose realistic timelines. Identify opportunities for team growth. Recommend contractor augmentation for peak periods.

### Emily Watson

- **Role:** Product Director
- **Influence:** Medium
- **Stance:** Neutral

**Interests:** Feature velocity and customer experience improvements. Worried migration will slow product delivery.

**Concerns:** 18-month migration feels too long. Wants visible customer benefits earlier.

**Strategy:** Include customer-facing improvements in early phases. Quantify performance and reliability benefits. Regular demos of new capabilities.

### James Park

- **Role:** Security Lead
- **Influence:** Medium
- **Stance:** Skeptic

**Interests:** Maintaining security posture during transition. New technologies introduce unknown risks.

**Concerns:** Cloud security model different from on-premise. Kubernetes adds complexity.

**Strategy:** Early involvement in architecture decisions. Security review gates at each phase. Training on cloud security patterns.

### David Chen

- **Role:** Finance Director
- **Influence:** Medium
- **Stance:** Skeptic

**Interests:** Cost control and ROI visibility. Migration budgets often overrun.

**Concerns:** AWS costs unpredictable. Oracle license savings may not materialize as planned.

**Strategy:** Monthly cost reporting with forecasts. Clear linkage between spend and capability delivery. Identify cost optimization opportunities.
